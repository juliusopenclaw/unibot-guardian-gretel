# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want UniBot Guardian to be a rigorous, respectful, and public-interest
research collaboration. Contributors should be able to challenge assumptions,
ask careful questions, and improve the evidence without personal attacks or
pressure to disclose private information.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind, precise, and source-oriented.
- Separate evidence from opinion.
- Respect privacy and institutional review boundaries.
- Prefer reproducible tests over rhetorical certainty.
- Welcome correction when a claim, source, or test is weak.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Publishing private or sensitive material.
- Harassment, insults, or discriminatory comments.
- Pressuring people to share personal, health, course, exam, or workplace data.
- Presenting UniBot as cleared for exams, grading, proctoring, AI-detection
  evidence, or accommodation decisions when it is not.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, reject, or remove contributions that break the project
boundary, weaken public safety, or make unsupported claims. Scientific rigor and
participant safety take priority over speed.
