# UniBot Open Science Roadmap

Status: public collaboration roadmap, not exam clearance.

## North Star

UniBot should be a reproducible, source-grounded, open-source research effort
for Socratic AI support in coding education. It should help students learn with
AI without hiding final answers inside the tool, and help universities review
the evidence, boundaries, and risks before any controlled use.

## Standards

- Three Golden Rules stay loaded in every loop.
- Source cards separate law, university policy, public guidance, technical
  documentation, research papers, and community leads.
- Claims need source anchors, uncertainty labels, and limitations.
- Synthetic evaluation comes before real student or course data.
- Public work uses public-safe fixtures only.
- Real pilots require ethics, data-protection, teaching, IT, inclusion, and
  exam-authority review before any participant or exam deployment.
- Model suggestions are proposals, not commits, sends, or release decisions.

## Build Chunks

1. Public repository hygiene: license, contribution guide, security policy,
   code of conduct, quickstart, and release gates.
2. Gretel/GLM proposal lane: redacted work packets, proposal validation,
   receipt hashes, and no-provider-call default.
3. Evidence and evaluation: source-card expansion, synthetic scenario registry,
   red-team cases, grader matrix, and public-safety checks.
4. Reproducible demos: local API, practice overlay, synthetic notebooks,
   public demo feedback, and issue-bundle drafts.
5. Institutional review: authority packet, compliance matrix, pilot protocol,
   privacy screening, review-board packet, and explicit not-cleared boundary.
6. Recursive self-improvement: every accepted or rejected idea becomes a test,
   source update, risk note, or documented blocker.

## Contributor Lanes

- `sources`: improve source cards and uncertainty notes.
- `evals`: add synthetic tasks, graders, and red-team cases.
- `privacy`: strengthen public-safety scans and data-minimisation rules.
- `pedagogy`: improve A0-A2 Socratic hints and reflection scaffolds.
- `accessibility`: keep support score-neutral and non-decisional.
- `docs`: make the system card, data card, runbook, and limitations easier to
  review.

## Non-Goals

- No proctoring.
- No AI detection as evidence.
- No automatic grading.
- No decision on accommodations.
- No real exam deployment without written authority clearance.
- No public release of private course material, raw model transcripts, local
  paths, personal data, or real exam work.
